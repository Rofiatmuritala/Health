About Us

Anemia is the most common hematologic abnormality identified in infants and children. Approximately a quarter of the world's population suffers from anemia, almost 2 billion people, with almost half of children < 5 years of age affected in 2016. Anemia is associated with increased morbidity and mortality in children, particularly children of preschool age. There are many causes of anemia, both inherited and acquired, and these causes vary widely in populations across the world. Anemia is not a specific disease entity per se but represents a heterogeneous group of pathologic conditions.
